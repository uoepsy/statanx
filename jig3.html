Teaching your students

Effective teaching is essential for maintaining student engagement and encouraging academic growth. By utilizing interactive lectures/demonstrations, facilitating hands-on individual/group activities, and by providing materials that students can work through/review at their own pace, instructors can effectively capture and sustain students' attention.

Relating teaching material to real-world examples and literature in the wider field may help to enhance student engagement. By illustrating how concepts apply in practical situations and connecting them to current research, instructors can make the material more meaningful and relevant.

Recognizing the heterogeneity of student learning preferences, instructors should aim to provide a range of learning opportunities via varied teaching methods/materials. These could include online resources, discussion forums, diverse reading lists, and hands-on activities to cater to the majority of students. As well as engaging in different teaching methods, instructors should aim to offer varied support options (e.g., 1:1 meetings, small group sessions, or large Q&A sessions) to allow students to feel comfortable seeking help in their preferred learning environment.

Creating an engaging and inclusive learning environment where students feel valued should ensure that instructors can foster a positive academic learning culture conducive to learning and growth.
